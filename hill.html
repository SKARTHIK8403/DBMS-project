India is blessed with numerous hill stations, offering respite from the scorching summer heat and providing breathtaking views of majestic mountains, lush greenery, and serene landscapes.

Best Hill Stations To Visit In India Are

  • Coorg
  • Munnar
  • Mount Abu
  • Nainital
  • Shimla

  • Coorg

  • Abbey Falls

    A scenic waterfall located amidst coffee plantations. It is a popular tourist attraction known for its natural beauty and tranquil surroundings.

    Timings: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 15 to INR 30 per person

  • Madikeri Fort

    Also known as Mercara Fort, it is a historical fort built by the erstwhile rulers of Coorg. The fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and houses a museum showcasing artifacts and relics of the region's history.

    Timings: 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 10 to INR 25 per person

  • Raja's Seat

    A beautiful garden and viewpoint located in Madikeri, offering breathtaking views of the sunset. It is a perfect spot for enjoying the scenic beauty of Coorg and relaxing amidst nature.

    Timings: 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 5 to INR 10 per person

  • Namdroling Monastery (Golden Temple)

    One of the largest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in India, known for its magnificent architecture, golden statues, and serene ambiance. Visitors can witness Buddhist rituals and teachings at the monastery.

    Timings: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM

    No Entry Fee, Donations welcome

  • Talakaveri

    The origin of the River Kaveri, located atop the Brahmagiri Hill. It is a sacred site for Hindus and offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

    Timings: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM

    No Entry Fee

  • Dubare Elephant Camp

    A popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Visitors can participate in elephant interactions, feeding, and bathing activities while learning about elephant conservation efforts.

    Timings: 8:30 AM to 11:00 AM, 4:30 PM to 5:30 PM

    Elephant Interaction Fee: Approximately INR 100 to INR 500 per person (varies based on activity)

  • Coffee Plantations

    Coorg is renowned for its coffee plantations, and a visit to these plantations offers a unique experience to learn about coffee cultivation, processing, and tasting. Many plantations also offer homestay accommodations for tourists.

    Timings: Visits usually arranged by prior appointment

    Guided Tour Fee: Approximately INR 200 to INR 500 per person (includes plantation tour and coffee tasting)

  • Trekking

    Coorg offers several trekking trails amidst its scenic landscapes and hills. Popular trekking destinations include Tadiandamol Peak, Brahmagiri Peak, and Pushpagiri Peak.

    Timings: Varies based on trek route and difficulty level

    Guide Fee (if required): Approximately INR 500 to INR 2000 per trek (depends on duration and route)

  • Raja's Tomb

    The final resting place of Kodava kings and queens, located in Madikeri. The tombs are built in Indo-Sarcenic style architecture and are surrounded by beautiful gardens.

    Timings: 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 10 to INR 25 per person

  • Munnar

  • Eravikulam National Park

    A protected area known for its endangered Nilgiri Tahr and rich biodiversity. Visitors can enjoy trekking and wildlife spotting amidst lush greenery.

    Timings: 7:00 AM to 4:00 PM (Closed during February and March)

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 125 to INR 420 per person (varies for Indian and foreign nationals)

  • Mattupetty Dam

    A concrete gravity dam built near the Anamudi Peak, offering scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Boating and picnicking are popular activities here.

    Timings: 9:30 AM to 5:30 PM

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 10 to INR 25 per person

  • Top Station

    Located at the highest point on the Munnar-Kodaikanal road, Top Station offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the valley of Theni district. It is a popular trekking destination and offers stunning sunrise views.

    Timings: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM

    Entry Fee: Free

  • Tea Gardens

    Munnar is renowned for its sprawling tea plantations, which offer scenic vistas of manicured greenery against the backdrop of misty mountains. Visitors can take guided tours of tea factories and learn about the tea-making process.

    Timings: Varies

    Entry Fee: Free

  • Attukal Waterfalls

    A mesmerizing waterfall located between Munnar and Pallivasal, surrounded by lush green hills and rocky cliffs. It is an ideal spot for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts.

    Timings: Open 24 hours

    Entry Fee: Free

  • Tea Museum

    The Tata Tea Museum showcases the history and evolution of tea plantations in Munnar. Visitors can explore exhibits on tea processing, machinery, and vintage photographs.

    Timings: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 75 per person

  • Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary

    A biodiversity hotspot located in the rain shadow region of the Western Ghats. It is home to diverse flora and fauna, including the endangered grizzled giant squirrel and the star tortoise.

    Timings: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM

    Entry Fee: Approximately INR 10 to INR 100 per person (varies for Indian and foreign nationals)

  • Marayoor Dolmens

    Located in Marayoor village, these ancient dolmens are believed to date back to the Neolithic period. They are stone structures with historical and archaeological significance.

    Timings: Open 24 hours

    Entry Fee: Free
